abstract: |
  Generative models can synthesize highly realistic content that is already being misused at scale to undermine digital media authenticity. Current deepfake detection methods are unreliable because distinguishing inauthentic content post-hoc is sometimes impossible, leading to unbounded false positive rates, and because detectors can be evaded by adaptive adversaries. We propose a resynthesis framework to determine if a sample is authentic or if its authenticity can be plausibly denied. Our calibrated resynthesis method is designed for high-precision, low-recall verification of authentic samples while maintaining controllable false positive rates, and it shows adversarial robustness against efficient attackers across modalities.
